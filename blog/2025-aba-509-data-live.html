The full 2011–2025 ABA 509 record is now live on every school page

Changelog · June 1, 2026

The full 2011–2025 ABA Standard 509 record is now live on every school page — not just the latest cycle. Here's what shipped, and why it matters if you're weighing a law degree.

Exhibit started as an interactive map. The data underneath always went back to 2011, but only the current cycle was visible to people (and search engines) who don't run the app. That's fixed.

What's new

  • 15-year trajectory on every school page. Each school page now shows a year-by-year table (LSAT, acceptance, bar passage, employment, tuition, grants) for 2011–2025 — the longitudinal view no other free source exposes.
  • Ranked answer pages. Highest bar passage, best value, cheapest, and most BigLaw — each ranked straight from the disclosures.
  • A hub for all 50 states. Every state now has a page ranking its schools, e.g. California and Texas.
  • One open dataset. The whole corpus is downloadable as exhibit-dataset.json, free, with a clear license.

Across the 197 currently-accredited schools, the median first-time bar pass rate is 83% and median resident tuition is $45,199 a year — but the spread is enormous, which is the whole point of looking school by school.
