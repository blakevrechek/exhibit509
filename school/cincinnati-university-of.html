University of Cincinnati School of Law

Ohio · Public · School ID: cincinnati-university-of
⚠ Data corrected. One or more figures on this page were adjudicated against the ABA source to make the series true. See how we handle anomalies.

University of Cincinnati School of Law is a public law school in Ohio. In the most recent ABA Standard 509 cycle it reported a 85.6% first-time bar passage rate (state average 80.6%). 91.3% of graduates landed full-time, long-term JD-required or JD-advantage jobs, and 14.9% joined large firms of 251+ attorneys. Resident tuition is $24,010 per year; a median grant of $19,000 brings median net tuition to about $5,010. The median LSAT is 159 with a 32.7% acceptance rate.

Frequently asked questions

What is the first-time bar passage rate at University of Cincinnati School of Law?

University of Cincinnati School of Law reported a 85.6% first-time bar passage rate in the most recent ABA Standard 509 disclosure, versus a 80.6% state average.

How much does University of Cincinnati School of Law cost?

Resident tuition at University of Cincinnati School of Law is $24,010 per year (about $72,030 over three years). The median grant is $19,000, bringing median net tuition to roughly $5,010 per year.

What LSAT and GPA do you need for University of Cincinnati School of Law?

The median (50th-percentile) LSAT at University of Cincinnati School of Law is 159 and the median GPA is 3.80; the acceptance rate is 32.7%.

What are the job outcomes at University of Cincinnati School of Law?

91.3% of University of Cincinnati School of Law graduates held full-time, long-term JD-required or JD-advantage jobs about ten months after graduation. 14.9% joined large firms of 251+ attorneys. 7.9% were still seeking employment.
