Case Western Reserve University School of Law

Ohio · Private · School ID: case-western-reserve-university

Case Western Reserve University School of Law is a private law school in Ohio. In the most recent ABA Standard 509 cycle it reported a 82.0% first-time bar passage rate (state average 81.0%). 81.7% of graduates landed full-time, long-term JD-required or JD-advantage jobs, and 20.5% joined large firms of 251+ attorneys. Resident tuition is $64,600 per year; a median grant of $42,500 brings median net tuition to about $22,100. The median LSAT is 162 with a 34.8% acceptance rate.

Frequently asked questions

What is the first-time bar passage rate at Case Western Reserve University School of Law?

Case Western Reserve University School of Law reported a 82.0% first-time bar passage rate in the most recent ABA Standard 509 disclosure, versus a 81.0% state average.

How much does Case Western Reserve University School of Law cost?

Resident tuition at Case Western Reserve University School of Law is $64,600 per year (about $193,800 over three years). The median grant is $42,500, bringing median net tuition to roughly $22,100 per year.

What LSAT and GPA do you need for Case Western Reserve University School of Law?

The median (50th-percentile) LSAT at Case Western Reserve University School of Law is 162 and the median GPA is 3.76; the acceptance rate is 34.8%.

What are the job outcomes at Case Western Reserve University School of Law?

81.7% of Case Western Reserve University School of Law graduates held full-time, long-term JD-required or JD-advantage jobs about ten months after graduation. 20.5% joined large firms of 251+ attorneys. 3.5% were still seeking employment.
