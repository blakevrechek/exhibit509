Barry University School of Law

Florida · Private · School ID: barry-university

Barry University School of Law is a private law school in Florida. In the most recent ABA Standard 509 cycle it reported a 68.6% first-time bar passage rate (state average 73.5%). 72.8% of graduates landed full-time, long-term JD-required or JD-advantage jobs, and 10.2% joined large firms of 251+ attorneys. Resident tuition is $42,000 per year; a median grant of $16,000 brings median net tuition to about $26,000. The median LSAT is 150 with a 55.9% acceptance rate.

Frequently asked questions

What is the first-time bar passage rate at Barry University School of Law?

Barry University School of Law reported a 68.6% first-time bar passage rate in the most recent ABA Standard 509 disclosure, versus a 73.5% state average.

How much does Barry University School of Law cost?

Resident tuition at Barry University School of Law is $42,000 per year (about $126,000 over three years). The median grant is $16,000, bringing median net tuition to roughly $26,000 per year.

What LSAT and GPA do you need for Barry University School of Law?

The median (50th-percentile) LSAT at Barry University School of Law is 150 and the median GPA is 3.23; the acceptance rate is 55.9%.

What are the job outcomes at Barry University School of Law?

72.8% of Barry University School of Law graduates held full-time, long-term JD-required or JD-advantage jobs about ten months after graduation. 10.2% joined large firms of 251+ attorneys. 10.2% were still seeking employment.
