Cornell Law School

New York · Private · School ID: cornell-university

Cornell Law School is a private law school in New York. In the most recent ABA Standard 509 cycle it reported a 96.4% first-time bar passage rate (state average 84.7%). 99.5% of graduates landed full-time, long-term JD-required or JD-advantage jobs, and 63.0% joined large firms of 251+ attorneys. Resident tuition is $84,722 per year; a median grant of $30,000 brings median net tuition to about $54,722. The median LSAT is 173 with a 18.2% acceptance rate.

Frequently asked questions

What is the first-time bar passage rate at Cornell Law School?

Cornell Law School reported a 96.4% first-time bar passage rate in the most recent ABA Standard 509 disclosure, versus a 84.7% state average.

How much does Cornell Law School cost?

Resident tuition at Cornell Law School is $84,722 per year (about $254,166 over three years). The median grant is $30,000, bringing median net tuition to roughly $54,722 per year.

What LSAT and GPA do you need for Cornell Law School?

The median (50th-percentile) LSAT at Cornell Law School is 173 and the median GPA is 3.92; the acceptance rate is 18.2%.

What are the job outcomes at Cornell Law School?

99.5% of Cornell Law School graduates held full-time, long-term JD-required or JD-advantage jobs about ten months after graduation. 63.0% joined large firms of 251+ attorneys. 0.0% were still seeking employment.
