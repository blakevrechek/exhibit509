University of California-San Francisco School of Law

California · Public · School ID: california-san-francisco-university-of

University of California-San Francisco School of Law is a public law school in California. In the most recent ABA Standard 509 cycle it reported a 83.8% first-time bar passage rate (state average 77.9%). 91.2% of graduates landed full-time, long-term JD-required or JD-advantage jobs, and 28.0% joined large firms of 251+ attorneys. Resident tuition is $53,087 per year; a median grant of $20,000 brings median net tuition to about $33,087. The median LSAT is 161 with a 27.5% acceptance rate.

Frequently asked questions

What is the first-time bar passage rate at University of California-San Francisco School of Law?

University of California-San Francisco School of Law reported a 83.8% first-time bar passage rate in the most recent ABA Standard 509 disclosure, versus a 77.9% state average.

How much does University of California-San Francisco School of Law cost?

Resident tuition at University of California-San Francisco School of Law is $53,087 per year (about $159,261 over three years). The median grant is $20,000, bringing median net tuition to roughly $33,087 per year.

What LSAT and GPA do you need for University of California-San Francisco School of Law?

The median (50th-percentile) LSAT at University of California-San Francisco School of Law is 161 and the median GPA is 3.71; the acceptance rate is 27.5%.

What are the job outcomes at University of California-San Francisco School of Law?

91.2% of University of California-San Francisco School of Law graduates held full-time, long-term JD-required or JD-advantage jobs about ten months after graduation. 28.0% joined large firms of 251+ attorneys. 1.4% were still seeking employment.
