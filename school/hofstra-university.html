Hofstra University School of Law

New York · Private · School ID: hofstra-university

Hofstra University School of Law is a private law school in New York. In the most recent ABA Standard 509 cycle it reported a 78.9% first-time bar passage rate (state average 85.7%). 90.1% of graduates landed full-time, long-term JD-required or JD-advantage jobs, and 13.3% joined large firms of 251+ attorneys. Resident tuition is $73,344 per year; a median grant of $35,793 brings median net tuition to about $37,551. The median LSAT is 157 with a 40.6% acceptance rate.

Frequently asked questions

What is the first-time bar passage rate at Hofstra University School of Law?

Hofstra University School of Law reported a 78.9% first-time bar passage rate in the most recent ABA Standard 509 disclosure, versus a 85.7% state average.

How much does Hofstra University School of Law cost?

Resident tuition at Hofstra University School of Law is $73,344 per year (about $220,032 over three years). The median grant is $35,793, bringing median net tuition to roughly $37,551 per year.

What LSAT and GPA do you need for Hofstra University School of Law?

The median (50th-percentile) LSAT at Hofstra University School of Law is 157 and the median GPA is 3.65; the acceptance rate is 40.6%.

What are the job outcomes at Hofstra University School of Law?

90.1% of Hofstra University School of Law graduates held full-time, long-term JD-required or JD-advantage jobs about ten months after graduation. 13.3% joined large firms of 251+ attorneys. 4.6% were still seeking employment.
