Widener University Commonwealth Law School

Pennsylvania · Private · School ID: widener-university-commonwealth-law-scho
⚠ Data corrected. One or more figures on this page were adjudicated against the ABA source to make the series true. See how we handle anomalies.

Widener University Commonwealth Law School is a private law school in Pennsylvania. In the most recent ABA Standard 509 cycle it reported a 54.0% first-time bar passage rate (state average 80.1%). 84.8% of graduates landed full-time, long-term JD-required or JD-advantage jobs, and 1.9% joined large firms of 251+ attorneys. Resident tuition is $54,375 per year; a median grant of $25,000 brings median net tuition to about $29,375. The median LSAT is 150 with a 60.4% acceptance rate.

Frequently asked questions

What is the first-time bar passage rate at Widener University Commonwealth Law School?

Widener University Commonwealth Law School reported a 54.0% first-time bar passage rate in the most recent ABA Standard 509 disclosure, versus a 80.1% state average.

How much does Widener University Commonwealth Law School cost?

Resident tuition at Widener University Commonwealth Law School is $54,375 per year (about $163,125 over three years). The median grant is $25,000, bringing median net tuition to roughly $29,375 per year.

What LSAT and GPA do you need for Widener University Commonwealth Law School?

The median (50th-percentile) LSAT at Widener University Commonwealth Law School is 150 and the median GPA is 3.33; the acceptance rate is 60.4%.

What are the job outcomes at Widener University Commonwealth Law School?

84.8% of Widener University Commonwealth Law School graduates held full-time, long-term JD-required or JD-advantage jobs about ten months after graduation. 1.9% joined large firms of 251+ attorneys. 12.4% were still seeking employment.
