University of Tennessee School of Law

Tennessee · Public · School ID: tennessee-university-of

University of Tennessee School of Law is a public law school in Tennessee. In the most recent ABA Standard 509 cycle it reported a 91.9% first-time bar passage rate (state average 78.5%). 92.7% of graduates landed full-time, long-term JD-required or JD-advantage jobs, and 13.7% joined large firms of 251+ attorneys. Resident tuition is $16,696 per year; a median grant of $10,550 brings median net tuition to about $6,146. The median LSAT is 164 with a 24.8% acceptance rate.

Frequently asked questions

What is the first-time bar passage rate at University of Tennessee School of Law?

University of Tennessee School of Law reported a 91.9% first-time bar passage rate in the most recent ABA Standard 509 disclosure, versus a 78.5% state average.

How much does University of Tennessee School of Law cost?

Resident tuition at University of Tennessee School of Law is $16,696 per year (about $50,088 over three years). The median grant is $10,550, bringing median net tuition to roughly $6,146 per year.

What LSAT and GPA do you need for University of Tennessee School of Law?

The median (50th-percentile) LSAT at University of Tennessee School of Law is 164 and the median GPA is 3.89; the acceptance rate is 24.8%.

What are the job outcomes at University of Tennessee School of Law?

92.7% of University of Tennessee School of Law graduates held full-time, long-term JD-required or JD-advantage jobs about ten months after graduation. 13.7% joined large firms of 251+ attorneys. 1.6% were still seeking employment.
