University of Hawaii School of Law

Hawaii · Public · School ID: hawaii-university-of

University of Hawaii School of Law is a public law school in Hawaii. In the most recent ABA Standard 509 cycle it reported a 76.9% first-time bar passage rate (state average 74.9%). 91.8% of graduates landed full-time, long-term JD-required or JD-advantage jobs. Resident tuition is $23,304 per year; a median grant of $7,000 brings median net tuition to about $16,304. The median LSAT is 158 with a 26.2% acceptance rate.

Frequently asked questions

What is the first-time bar passage rate at University of Hawaii School of Law?

University of Hawaii School of Law reported a 76.9% first-time bar passage rate in the most recent ABA Standard 509 disclosure, versus a 74.9% state average.

How much does University of Hawaii School of Law cost?

Resident tuition at University of Hawaii School of Law is $23,304 per year (about $69,912 over three years). The median grant is $7,000, bringing median net tuition to roughly $16,304 per year.

What LSAT and GPA do you need for University of Hawaii School of Law?

The median (50th-percentile) LSAT at University of Hawaii School of Law is 158 and the median GPA is 3.62; the acceptance rate is 26.2%.

What are the job outcomes at University of Hawaii School of Law?

91.8% of University of Hawaii School of Law graduates held full-time, long-term JD-required or JD-advantage jobs about ten months after graduation. 5.1% were still seeking employment.
