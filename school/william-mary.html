William & Mary School of Law

Virginia · Public · School ID: william-&-mary

William & Mary School of Law is a public law school in Virginia. In the most recent ABA Standard 509 cycle it reported a 83.6% first-time bar passage rate (state average 80.0%). 93.5% of graduates landed full-time, long-term JD-required or JD-advantage jobs, and 23.6% joined large firms of 251+ attorneys. Resident tuition is $38,734 per year; a median grant of $29,302 brings median net tuition to about $9,432. The median LSAT is 166 with a 22.0% acceptance rate.

Frequently asked questions

What is the first-time bar passage rate at William & Mary School of Law?

William & Mary School of Law reported a 83.6% first-time bar passage rate in the most recent ABA Standard 509 disclosure, versus a 80.0% state average.

How much does William & Mary School of Law cost?

Resident tuition at William & Mary School of Law is $38,734 per year (about $116,202 over three years). The median grant is $29,302, bringing median net tuition to roughly $9,432 per year.

What LSAT and GPA do you need for William & Mary School of Law?

The median (50th-percentile) LSAT at William & Mary School of Law is 166 and the median GPA is 3.82; the acceptance rate is 22.0%.

What are the job outcomes at William & Mary School of Law?

93.5% of William & Mary School of Law graduates held full-time, long-term JD-required or JD-advantage jobs about ten months after graduation. 23.6% joined large firms of 251+ attorneys. 3.0% were still seeking employment.
