University of New Mexico School of Law

New Mexico · Public · School ID: new-mexico-the-university-of

University of New Mexico School of Law is a public law school in New Mexico. In the most recent ABA Standard 509 cycle it reported a 85.7% first-time bar passage rate (state average 81.2%). 78.0% of graduates landed full-time, long-term JD-required or JD-advantage jobs. Resident tuition is $19,474 per year; a median grant of $10,000 brings median net tuition to about $9,474. The median LSAT is 157 with a 41.4% acceptance rate.

Frequently asked questions

What is the first-time bar passage rate at University of New Mexico School of Law?

University of New Mexico School of Law reported a 85.7% first-time bar passage rate in the most recent ABA Standard 509 disclosure, versus a 81.2% state average.

How much does University of New Mexico School of Law cost?

Resident tuition at University of New Mexico School of Law is $19,474 per year (about $58,422 over three years). The median grant is $10,000, bringing median net tuition to roughly $9,474 per year.

What LSAT and GPA do you need for University of New Mexico School of Law?

The median (50th-percentile) LSAT at University of New Mexico School of Law is 157 and the median GPA is 3.56; the acceptance rate is 41.4%.

What are the job outcomes at University of New Mexico School of Law?

78.0% of University of New Mexico School of Law graduates held full-time, long-term JD-required or JD-advantage jobs about ten months after graduation. 1.0% were still seeking employment.
