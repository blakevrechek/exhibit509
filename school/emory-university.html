Emory University School of Law

Georgia · Private · School ID: emory-university

Emory University School of Law is a private law school in Georgia. In the most recent ABA Standard 509 cycle it reported a 88.4% first-time bar passage rate (state average 80.5%). 91.1% of graduates landed full-time, long-term JD-required or JD-advantage jobs, and 35.3% joined large firms of 251+ attorneys. Resident tuition is $69,510 per year; a median grant of $36,000 brings median net tuition to about $33,510. The median LSAT is 166 with a 38.7% acceptance rate.

Frequently asked questions

What is the first-time bar passage rate at Emory University School of Law?

Emory University School of Law reported a 88.4% first-time bar passage rate in the most recent ABA Standard 509 disclosure, versus a 80.5% state average.

How much does Emory University School of Law cost?

Resident tuition at Emory University School of Law is $69,510 per year (about $208,530 over three years). The median grant is $36,000, bringing median net tuition to roughly $33,510 per year.

What LSAT and GPA do you need for Emory University School of Law?

The median (50th-percentile) LSAT at Emory University School of Law is 166 and the median GPA is 3.82; the acceptance rate is 38.7%.

What are the job outcomes at Emory University School of Law?

91.1% of Emory University School of Law graduates held full-time, long-term JD-required or JD-advantage jobs about ten months after graduation. 35.3% joined large firms of 251+ attorneys. 4.5% were still seeking employment.
