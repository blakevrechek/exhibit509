Florida A&M University School of Law

Florida · Public · School ID: florida-a&m-university

Florida A&M University School of Law is a public law school in Florida. In the most recent ABA Standard 509 cycle it reported a 75.4% first-time bar passage rate (state average 73.3%). 79.6% of graduates landed full-time, long-term JD-required or JD-advantage jobs, and 7.4% joined large firms of 251+ attorneys. Resident tuition is $13,224 per year; a median grant of $7,500 brings median net tuition to about $5,724. The median LSAT is 152 with a 34.7% acceptance rate.

Frequently asked questions

What is the first-time bar passage rate at Florida A&M University School of Law?

Florida A&M University School of Law reported a 75.4% first-time bar passage rate in the most recent ABA Standard 509 disclosure, versus a 73.3% state average.

How much does Florida A&M University School of Law cost?

Resident tuition at Florida A&M University School of Law is $13,224 per year (about $39,672 over three years). The median grant is $7,500, bringing median net tuition to roughly $5,724 per year.

What LSAT and GPA do you need for Florida A&M University School of Law?

The median (50th-percentile) LSAT at Florida A&M University School of Law is 152 and the median GPA is 3.48; the acceptance rate is 34.7%.

What are the job outcomes at Florida A&M University School of Law?

79.6% of Florida A&M University School of Law graduates held full-time, long-term JD-required or JD-advantage jobs about ten months after graduation. 7.4% joined large firms of 251+ attorneys. 8.3% were still seeking employment.
