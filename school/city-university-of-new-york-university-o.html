City University of New York School of Law

New York · Public · School ID: city-university-of-new-york-university-o

City University of New York School of Law is a public law school in New York. In the most recent ABA Standard 509 cycle it reported a 65.7% first-time bar passage rate (state average 86.0%). 80.7% of graduates landed full-time, long-term JD-required or JD-advantage jobs, and 2.0% joined large firms of 251+ attorneys. Resident tuition is $15,450 per year; a median grant of $8,000 brings median net tuition to about $7,450. The median LSAT is 155 with a 28.6% acceptance rate.

Frequently asked questions

What is the first-time bar passage rate at City University of New York School of Law?

City University of New York School of Law reported a 65.7% first-time bar passage rate in the most recent ABA Standard 509 disclosure, versus a 86.0% state average.

How much does City University of New York School of Law cost?

Resident tuition at City University of New York School of Law is $15,450 per year (about $46,350 over three years). The median grant is $8,000, bringing median net tuition to roughly $7,450 per year.

What LSAT and GPA do you need for City University of New York School of Law?

The median (50th-percentile) LSAT at City University of New York School of Law is 155 and the median GPA is 3.54; the acceptance rate is 28.6%.

What are the job outcomes at City University of New York School of Law?

80.7% of City University of New York School of Law graduates held full-time, long-term JD-required or JD-advantage jobs about ten months after graduation. 2.0% joined large firms of 251+ attorneys. 10.4% were still seeking employment.
