Ave Maria School of Law

Florida · Private · School ID: ave-maria-school-of-law

Ave Maria School of Law is a private law school in Florida. In the most recent ABA Standard 509 cycle it reported a 65.9% first-time bar passage rate (state average 73.8%). 84.0% of graduates landed full-time, long-term JD-required or JD-advantage jobs, and 4.9% joined large firms of 251+ attorneys. Resident tuition is $49,500 per year; a median grant of $30,000 brings median net tuition to about $19,500. The median LSAT is 155 with a 47.0% acceptance rate.

Frequently asked questions

What is the first-time bar passage rate at Ave Maria School of Law?

Ave Maria School of Law reported a 65.9% first-time bar passage rate in the most recent ABA Standard 509 disclosure, versus a 73.8% state average.

How much does Ave Maria School of Law cost?

Resident tuition at Ave Maria School of Law is $49,500 per year (about $148,500 over three years). The median grant is $30,000, bringing median net tuition to roughly $19,500 per year.

What LSAT and GPA do you need for Ave Maria School of Law?

The median (50th-percentile) LSAT at Ave Maria School of Law is 155 and the median GPA is 3.36; the acceptance rate is 47.0%.

What are the job outcomes at Ave Maria School of Law?

84.0% of Ave Maria School of Law graduates held full-time, long-term JD-required or JD-advantage jobs about ten months after graduation. 4.9% joined large firms of 251+ attorneys. 7.4% were still seeking employment.
