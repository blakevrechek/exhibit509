Seton Hall University School of Law

New Jersey · Private · School ID: seton-hall-university

Seton Hall University School of Law is a private law school in New Jersey. In the most recent ABA Standard 509 cycle it reported a 87.2% first-time bar passage rate (state average 75.7%). 95.7% of graduates landed full-time, long-term JD-required or JD-advantage jobs, and 17.9% joined large firms of 251+ attorneys. Resident tuition is $69,660 per year; a median grant of $40,000 brings median net tuition to about $29,660. The median LSAT is 161 with a 34.3% acceptance rate.

Frequently asked questions

What is the first-time bar passage rate at Seton Hall University School of Law?

Seton Hall University School of Law reported a 87.2% first-time bar passage rate in the most recent ABA Standard 509 disclosure, versus a 75.7% state average.

How much does Seton Hall University School of Law cost?

Resident tuition at Seton Hall University School of Law is $69,660 per year (about $208,980 over three years). The median grant is $40,000, bringing median net tuition to roughly $29,660 per year.

What LSAT and GPA do you need for Seton Hall University School of Law?

The median (50th-percentile) LSAT at Seton Hall University School of Law is 161 and the median GPA is 3.71; the acceptance rate is 34.3%.

What are the job outcomes at Seton Hall University School of Law?

95.7% of Seton Hall University School of Law graduates held full-time, long-term JD-required or JD-advantage jobs about ten months after graduation. 17.9% joined large firms of 251+ attorneys. 1.9% were still seeking employment.
