Cardozo, Yeshiva University School of Law

New York · Private · School ID: cardozo-yeshiva-university

Cardozo, Yeshiva University School of Law is a private law school in New York. In the most recent ABA Standard 509 cycle it reported a 88.5% first-time bar passage rate (state average 85.2%). 90.8% of graduates landed full-time, long-term JD-required or JD-advantage jobs, and 25.1% joined large firms of 251+ attorneys. Resident tuition is $74,438 per year; a median grant of $40,000 brings median net tuition to about $34,438. The median LSAT is 165 with a 29.6% acceptance rate.

Frequently asked questions

What is the first-time bar passage rate at Cardozo, Yeshiva University School of Law?

Cardozo, Yeshiva University School of Law reported a 88.5% first-time bar passage rate in the most recent ABA Standard 509 disclosure, versus a 85.2% state average.

How much does Cardozo, Yeshiva University School of Law cost?

Resident tuition at Cardozo, Yeshiva University School of Law is $74,438 per year (about $223,314 over three years). The median grant is $40,000, bringing median net tuition to roughly $34,438 per year.

What LSAT and GPA do you need for Cardozo, Yeshiva University School of Law?

The median (50th-percentile) LSAT at Cardozo, Yeshiva University School of Law is 165 and the median GPA is 3.78; the acceptance rate is 29.6%.

What are the job outcomes at Cardozo, Yeshiva University School of Law?

90.8% of Cardozo, Yeshiva University School of Law graduates held full-time, long-term JD-required or JD-advantage jobs about ten months after graduation. 25.1% joined large firms of 251+ attorneys. 3.3% were still seeking employment.
