Mitchell/Hamline School of Law

Minnesota · Private · School ID: mitchell/hamline-school-of-law

Mitchell/Hamline School of Law is a private law school in Minnesota. In the most recent ABA Standard 509 cycle it reported a 71.0% first-time bar passage rate (state average 81.6%). 83.7% of graduates landed full-time, long-term JD-required or JD-advantage jobs, and 1.5% joined large firms of 251+ attorneys. Resident tuition is $55,300 per year; a median grant of $34,730 brings median net tuition to about $20,570. The median LSAT is 154 with a 51.4% acceptance rate.

Frequently asked questions

What is the first-time bar passage rate at Mitchell/Hamline School of Law?

Mitchell/Hamline School of Law reported a 71.0% first-time bar passage rate in the most recent ABA Standard 509 disclosure, versus a 81.6% state average.

How much does Mitchell/Hamline School of Law cost?

Resident tuition at Mitchell/Hamline School of Law is $55,300 per year (about $165,900 over three years). The median grant is $34,730, bringing median net tuition to roughly $20,570 per year.

What LSAT and GPA do you need for Mitchell/Hamline School of Law?

The median (50th-percentile) LSAT at Mitchell/Hamline School of Law is 154 and the median GPA is 3.29; the acceptance rate is 51.4%.

What are the job outcomes at Mitchell/Hamline School of Law?

83.7% of Mitchell/Hamline School of Law graduates held full-time, long-term JD-required or JD-advantage jobs about ten months after graduation. 1.5% joined large firms of 251+ attorneys. 7.7% were still seeking employment.
