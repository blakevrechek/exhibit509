Pontifical Catholic University of Puerto Rico School of Law

Puerto Rico · Private · School ID: pontifical-catholic-university-of-puerto

Pontifical Catholic University of Puerto Rico School of Law is a private law school in Puerto Rico. In the most recent ABA Standard 509 cycle it reported a 54.9% first-time bar passage rate (state average 52.1%). 38.8% of graduates landed full-time, long-term JD-required or JD-advantage jobs. Resident tuition is $15,660 per year; a median grant of $1,593 brings median net tuition to about $14,067. The median LSAT is 139 with a 61.5% acceptance rate.

Frequently asked questions

What is the first-time bar passage rate at Pontifical Catholic University of Puerto Rico School of Law?

Pontifical Catholic University of Puerto Rico School of Law reported a 54.9% first-time bar passage rate in the most recent ABA Standard 509 disclosure, versus a 52.1% state average.

How much does Pontifical Catholic University of Puerto Rico School of Law cost?

Resident tuition at Pontifical Catholic University of Puerto Rico School of Law is $15,660 per year (about $46,980 over three years). The median grant is $1,593, bringing median net tuition to roughly $14,067 per year.

What LSAT and GPA do you need for Pontifical Catholic University of Puerto Rico School of Law?

The median (50th-percentile) LSAT at Pontifical Catholic University of Puerto Rico School of Law is 139 and the median GPA is 3.33; the acceptance rate is 61.5%.

What are the job outcomes at Pontifical Catholic University of Puerto Rico School of Law?

38.8% of Pontifical Catholic University of Puerto Rico School of Law graduates held full-time, long-term JD-required or JD-advantage jobs about ten months after graduation. 29.3% were still seeking employment.
